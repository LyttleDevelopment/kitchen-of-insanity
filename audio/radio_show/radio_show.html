Orpheus Feeling

In late 1992, Paul De Borger was in a Belgian radio studio
to promote the then new album Orpheus Feeling.

For an independent band like Kitchen of Insanity, these
50 minutes on national radio were minutes well spent...
A chance to be heard nationwide...
Aiming at acknowledgement...
In search of growth and respect...

In this very insightful interview Paul talks about the band,
their music, influences, means of inspiration, the rather
unfortunate comparisons to The Doors and, above all, his
admiration for the late singer/songwriter Nick Drake.

Rescued from aging analog tapes and enriched with the
once cut-out songs, this really is essential listening
for fans and everyone who's been inspired by the album!

I'm terribly sorry for all who do not master the Dutch
language... Please refer to the Biography page to get
an idea of what's inside...
